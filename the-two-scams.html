The Two Scams

How one victim was defrauded twice by the same criminal network — and how blockchain analysis proved the connection

Overview

A single victim was defrauded twice by the same criminal organization. First through a romance/pig-butchering scam in July 2022, then through a fake cryptocurrency exchange in July 2024. Blockchain analysis proved both thefts trace to the same scammer wallet, establishing that one criminal network operated both schemes.

This investigation began with those two thefts and expanded through blockchain tracing, corporate records, domain analysis, and open-source intelligence into a multinational fraud operation spanning the United States, Canada, United Kingdom, Hong Kong, Latvia, France, Finland, Germany, and Singapore.

What Is deptoy.co / BIPPAX?

BIPPAX is a white-label fake exchange platform operated under multiple brand names. Each deployment receives a unique instance code (e.g., "BIPPAX-185"). The platform was incorporated in Canada as BISSNEX CRYPTO GROUP LIMITED (previously BIPPAX CRYPTO GROUP LIMITED, and before that ALPHA TECHNOLOGY HOLDING LIMITED). The company was dissolved in July 2025 after non-compliance, and its FINTRAC money services business registration expired in August 2025.

The Connection: One Wallet, Two Thefts

CRITICAL FINDING — Same Criminal Organization Behind Both Thefts
The scammer wallet 3GH4EhMi1MG8rxSiAWqfoiUCMLaWPTCxuy is the link between both incidents:
  • Incident 1 (2022): Victim's stolen 0.848 BTC reached this wallet via a 2-hop relay chain.
  • Incident 2 (July 2024): The deptoy.co/BIPPAX aggregation wallet sent 140 BTC in 7 direct transactions to this same wallet.

Only the holder of the private key for this wallet can receive funds at that address. The same person or group stole from this victim in 2022 and again in July 2024 through a different platform.

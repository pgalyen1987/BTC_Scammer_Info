How This Investigation Began

This investigation started with two separate theft incidents — one in 2022, one in 2024 — that on-chain analysis revealed were connected to the same criminal infrastructure. The 2024 incident directly identified the deptoy.co fake exchange platform, which became the thread that unravelled the broader network documented here.

Incident 1 — July 2022

Bitcoin Theft via Pig-Butchering Scam

A victim's Bitcoin wallet was swept in a single automated transaction with 41 outputs — a laundering technique designed to fragment funds and complicate tracing. The stolen funds passed through a relay chain before landing in an aggregator wallet that forwarded 97.25 BTC to Binance across 34 transactions.

Co-input analysis established that the aggregator wallet and the master scammer wallet (3GH4EhMi1MG8rxSiAWqfoiUCMLaWPTCxuy) share common ownership.

Incident 2 — July 2024

deptoy.co Fake Exchange — $10,040 Stolen

A victim deposited BTC from their Robinhood account into deptoy.co — a fake cryptocurrency exchange operating under the BIPPAX brand. The platform appeared legitimate but immediately swept all deposits into the BIPPAX aggregation wallet, which routed funds to the same master scammer wallet identified in the 2022 incident — proving both thefts were carried out by the same criminal operation.

The BIPPAX aggregation wallet has a 64,255 BTC lifetime balance across 25,117 transactions, indicating this platform has victimised many people beyond these two incidents.

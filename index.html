What This Network Is

This investigation documents a multi-operation pig-butchering (sha zhu pan) fraud network characterized by Chinese-origin technology, a Southeast Asian operator nexus, and globally distributed victims. The network spans at least three distinct but infrastructurally overlapping criminal operations, all sharing core technical fingerprints: CTG Server Limited HK hosting, SHARE-DNS.COM nameservers, and Gname.com as registrar. The underlying scam platforms originate from Chinese-language pig-butchering-as-a-service (PBaaS) kits sold by suppliers in mainland China and Taiwan.

Three distinct operations have been identified and partially attributed. fmtcapitaltc.com (the FMT Capital operation) is a Malaysian-deployed UWORK kit launched in early 2026, registered via Registrar.eu/OpenProvider and hosted behind Cloudflare on the CTG HK server. 2139exchange.com and its associated brand Mega Investment Group (megat.vip) is the largest operation by victim count, serving the Italian and broader European market, CONSOB-blacklisted since September 2024, and confirmed linked to the AZTEX criminal infrastructure via shared Porkbun nameserver fingerprint. BIPPAX / deptoy.co is a smaller operation linked to Darryl Joel Dorfman (Broomfield, CO), using a Canadian shell company (BISSNEX CRYPTO GROUP LIMITED), now defunct; the victim CRM and platform code was deployed as dacm-crypto.top on a Russian server (Selectel SPB).

All three operations share overlapping infrastructure: the CTG Server HK cluster (202.79.174.5) hosts both the Nanolite Foundation (Dorfman) and fmtcapitaltc.com, and all CTG-hosted domains use SHARE-DNS.COM nameservers registered at Gname.com. The broader geopolitical context is relevant: March 2026 congressional testimony (Reva Price, US-China Economic & Security Review Commission) confirmed that Beijing provides implicit backing for SE Asian compound operations; the "Chinese don't scam Chinese" targeting philosophy is confirmed in this network's domain strategy, which focuses typosquats on US, European, and Japanese financial institutions while avoiding Chinese domestic brands.

How We Got Here

The corporate trail (Page 3) revealed the shell companies used as Western fronts. Investigating the domains associated with those companies — and the scam platforms themselves — led to two distinct infrastructure clusters: the AZTEX operational infrastructure (European-based, running the BIPPAX platform backend) and the Hong Kong scam server farm (Chinese-based, hosting dozens of financial impersonation sites including Nanolite Foundation). Domain registration fingerprinting then revealed these connect through a shared DNS infrastructure (SHARE-DNS) operated by a single account at Gname.com in Singapore.

Binance Attribution — The Path to Real Identity

WHY BINANCE MATTERS
Binance is a regulated exchange that requires Know Your Customer (KYC) verification. Every account holder must submit government-issued ID, full name, date of birth, and address. When the aggregator wallet deposited 97.25 BTC across 34 transactions into Binance, the receiving account's real identity was recorded. A US subpoena to Binance (18 USC §2703) will reveal:
  • Account holder name, date of birth, nationality
  • KYC documents (passport or government ID)
  • Email address and phone number
  • Registration and login IP addresses
  • All deposit/withdrawal history
  • Linked bank accounts and external wallet addresses

The subject wallet also transacted with two addresses that match public Binance hot-wallet fingerprints in our trace — bc1qm34lsc65zpw79lxes69zkqmk6ee3ewf0j77s3h (472.52 BTC across 2,173,077 txs) and bc1quhruqrghgcca950rvhtrg7cpd7u8k6svpzgzmrjy8xyukacl5lkq0r8l2d (436.66 BTC) — supporting exchange subpoenas if those fingerprints are accepted by the venue.

Key Finding — New Scam Domain Identified

bravoex.info is a previously undocumented fake cryptocurrency exchange that sent a fraudulent deposit confirmation to the same victim (pgalyen87@gmail.com) targeted in the July 2024 deptoy.co incident. The email was sent on August 21, 2024 — approximately one month after the deptoy.co theft — claiming 13,597.15 USDT had been credited. The domain used Microsoft 365 infrastructure based in the Asia-Pacific region, matching the operational pattern of China-based pig-butchering networks. GoDaddy TLS certificates were active from March 2024 through June 2025. The domain is now dead (NXDOMAIN) — identical lifecycle to deptoy.co, bissnex.com, and other domains in this network.

Scam Indicators

Seven distinct forensic indicators confirm this email is part of a pig-butchering scam operation.

HIGH
Fake crypto deposit claim
Claims 13,597.15 USDT was deposited — designed to create urgency and compel the victim to “log in” to the fake exchange platform, where they would be prompted to deposit real funds or provide credentials.
HIGH
Message-ID contains @localhost
The Message-ID 827161167.605.1724199394369@localhost reveals the email was generated by an automated Java application (Jakarta Mail pattern), not sent from a real email client. The timestamp-based format is characteristic of mass-mailing scripts used in scam operations.
HIGH
Fake exchange language pattern
Uses “deposit,” “credited,” and “balance” — standard pig-butchering notification language designed to make the victim believe a deposit was made to their account on the fake exchange.
MEDIUM
Suspicious .info TLD
The .info TLD is commonly used by scam operations because it is inexpensive, has minimal registration requirements, and is rarely used by legitimate financial institutions.
MEDIUM
Urgency language
“Please log in to check your balance” creates urgency to access the platform, where the victim is then trapped in withdrawal fee scams.
MEDIUM
UTC+8 (China Standard Time) origin
Email was composed at 08:16 local time in the UTC+8 timezone. This is consistent with China-based pig-butchering operations that begin their workday messaging victims during US evening hours (17:16 PDT).
LOW
Minimal HTML body
Only 315 characters with zero links. Legitimate exchange notification emails contain branding, footers, unsubscribe links, and regulatory disclaimers. This bare-bones message is typical of scam notification emails.

Why SPF/DKIM/DMARC all pass

Pig-butchering operations deliberately configure proper email authentication because it ensures their scam emails reach victim inboxes without spam filtering. A paid Microsoft 365 subscription automatically provides SPF/DKIM signing, making scam emails appear indistinguishable from legitimate corporate email. Passing authentication does NOT indicate legitimacy — it only means the scammers properly configured their infrastructure.
